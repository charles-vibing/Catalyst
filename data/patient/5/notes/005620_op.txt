INDICATIONS:
Mr. Porter is a 79-year-old male with a displaced right intertrochanteric femur fracture after a mechanical fall at home. Given fracture pattern and patient age, he was indicated for operative fixation with open reduction and internal fixation using a sliding hip screw construct. Risks, benefits, and alternatives were discussed with the patient and family. Informed consent was obtained.

FINDINGS:
Displaced unstable intertrochanteric fracture of the right proximal femur. Cartilage of the acetabulum intact. No evidence of pathologic fracture. Soft tissues without gross purulence.

DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE:
The patient was brought to the operating room and placed supine on the fracture table. After successful induction of general anesthesia, a timeout was performed confirming correct patient, site (right hip), procedure, and implants. The right lower extremity was prepped and draped in standard sterile fashion. Prophylactic IV cefazolin was administered prior to incision per hospital protocol.

A standard lateral approach to the proximal femur was used. The fascia lata was incised and the vastus lateralis was elevated. The fracture site was exposed and hematoma evacuated.

The fracture was reduced under fluoroscopic guidance using traction and rotation. A guidewire was placed across the femoral neck. A cannulated sliding hip screw was inserted over the guidewire with satisfactory position confirmed on fluoroscopy. A 4-hole side plate was secured to the lateral femoral cortex with cortical screws. Final fluoroscopic images demonstrated anatomic reduction with appropriate screw position in the femoral head and stable plate fixation.

The wound was irrigated copiously with normal saline. The fascia and subcutaneous tissues were closed in layers with absorbable suture. Skin was closed with staples. A sterile dressing was applied.

The patient was extubated in the operating room and transferred to the PACU in stable condition tolerating the procedure well.
