DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE:
The patient was brought to the operating room and placed in the left lateral decubitus position. After adequate spinal anesthesia, the left hip was prepped and draped in sterile fashion. A standard posterior approach to the left hip was made. The fascia lata and gluteus maximus were split. The short external rotators were identified and divided. The hip was dislocated posteriorly. The femoral neck was osteotomized at the appropriate level. The femoral head was removed. The acetabulum was inspected and found to be intact. The femoral canal was sequentially broached to accept a size 4 cemented femoral stem. A trial reduction was performed with a 46 mm bipolar head; stability and range of motion were satisfactory. The final components were cemented in place. The hip was reduced. The wound was irrigated and closed in layers over a drain. Sterile dressing applied.
