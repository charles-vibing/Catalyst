INDICATIONS:
Ms. O'Brien is an 85-year-old female with a displaced right intertrochanteric femur fracture after a mechanical fall at home. Given fracture pattern, patient age, and comorbidities, she was indicated for operative fixation with cephalomedullary intramedullary nail rather than arthroplasty. Risks, benefits, and alternatives were discussed with the patient and family. Informed consent was obtained.

FINDINGS:
Displaced unstable intertrochanteric fracture of the right proximal femur. Cartilage of the acetabulum intact. No evidence of pathologic fracture. Soft tissues without gross purulence.

DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE:
The patient was brought to the operating room and placed supine on the fracture table. After successful induction of general anesthesia, a timeout was performed confirming correct patient, site (right hip), procedure, and implants. The right lower extremity was prepped and draped in standard sterile fashion. Prophylactic IV cefazolin was administered prior to incision per hospital protocol.

A percutaneous approach to the proximal femur was used with guidewire placement under fluoroscopic guidance. The guidewire was advanced across the fracture site into the femoral head. Sequential reaming was performed. A cephalomedullary nail of appropriate length was inserted over the guidewire and seated with satisfactory reduction and stable fixation of the intertrochanteric fracture. Proximal and distal locking screws were placed under fluoroscopy with good alignment and appropriate screw position in the femoral head and shaft.

Final fluoroscopic images demonstrated anatomic alignment, stable fracture fixation, and no intra-articular hardware penetration. Limb length was assessed and found to be appropriate compared to the contralateral side.

The wound was irrigated copiously with normal saline. The fascia and subcutaneous tissues were closed in layers with absorbable suture. Skin was closed with staples. A sterile dressing was applied.

The patient was extubated in the operating room and transferred to the PACU in stable condition tolerating the procedure well.
