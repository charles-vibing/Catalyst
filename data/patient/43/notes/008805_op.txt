DESCRIPTION OF PROCEDURE:
After obtaining informed consent, the patient was brought to the operating room and placed in the supine position on a fracture table. General endotracheal anesthesia was induced. The right lower extremity was prepped and draped in sterile fashion. A standard posterior approach to the right hip was made. The fascia was incised, and the short external rotators were released. The hip was dislocated posteriorly. The femoral neck was osteotomized at the appropriate level. The femoral head was removed. The acetabulum was inspected and found to be intact. The femoral canal was sequentially broached. A trial component was placed and reduced; stability and range of motion were satisfactory. The final cemented femoral stem and bipolar head were implanted. The hip was reduced. The wound was irrigated and closed in layers over a drain. Sterile dressings were applied.
